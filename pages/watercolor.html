Wet-on-Wet Technique

Beginner

Wet-on-Wet Basics

This is one of the most popular watercolor techniques where you apply wet paint to a pre-wetted surface. This creates soft, flowing, and blended effects that are characteristic of watercolor painting.

What You Need:

  • Watercolor paper (cold-pressed or rough)
  • Spray bottle or sponge for wetting
  • Watercolor paints
  • Soft, large brushes
  • Clean water

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Wet your paper evenly with clean water
  2. Apply pigment to the wet surface
  3. Watch as colors flow and blend naturally
  4. Add more colors while wet for seamless transitions
  5. Allow to dry naturally for best results

Dry Brush Technique

Intermediate

Mastering Dry Brush

The dry brush technique uses a minimal amount of water and paint to create texture and detail. It's perfect for adding finishing touches and creating contrast in your watercolor paintings.

Materials Required:

  • Watercolor paper
  • Medium-sized brushes with stiff bristles
  • Concentrated watercolor paints
  • Minimal water

Dry Brush Application:

  1. Load your brush with paint and minimal water
  2. Test on a scrap paper first
  3. Apply with quick, confident strokes
  4. Let the brush skip over the paper for texture
  5. Build up layers for more dramatic effects
  6. Perfect for creating tree foliage and rough textures

Layering & Glazing

Advanced

Building Depth with Layers

Watercolor layering involves applying transparent washes over previous layers to build color depth and complexity. This technique relies on the natural transparency of watercolors.

Key Principles:

  • Work light to dark colors
  • Allow each layer to dry completely
  • Use transparent colors for best results
  • Preserve white paper for highlights
  • Each layer should be lighter than intended final color

Layering Process:

  1. Start with lightest color washes
  2. Dry completely between layers
  3. Add medium-toned glazes
  4. Finish with darker accent colors
  5. Use masking tape if needed to preserve whites
  6. Build 3-5 layers for rich, complex colors

Watercolor Tips & Tricks

Paper Quality

Use quality watercolor paper (100% cotton rag) for best results and longevity.

Water Control

Water is your friend! Control it carefully to achieve the effects you want.

Value Matters

Pay attention to light and dark values to create strong compositions.

Happy Accidents

Embrace unexpected results - they often lead to unique and beautiful effects.
