Soft Pastels

Beginner

Working with Soft Pastels

Soft pastels offer vibrant colors and buttery texture, making them perfect for beginners. They blend easily and produce rich, luminous artwork.

Essential Materials:

  • Soft pastel sticks
  • Pastel paper or specialty surface
  • Blending stumps and tortillons
  • Kneaded eraser
  • Fixative spray
  • Soft brushes for blending

Soft Pastel Technique:

  1. Choose suitable pastel paper (medium tooth surface)
  2. Start with light colors first
  3. Apply pastels with gentle pressure
  4. Blend with blending stumps or soft brushes
  5. Layer colors for depth and richness
  6. Use kneaded eraser for corrections
  7. Seal finished work with fixative

Chalk Pastels

Beginner

Exploring Chalk Pastels

Chalk pastels are affordable, accessible, and great for learning pastel techniques. They blend smoothly and work well for large coverage areas.

What You Need:

  • Chalk pastel sets
  • Textured paper
  • Blending tools
  • Paper towels or rags
  • Protective spray

Working with Chalk Pastels:

  1. Select paper with enough texture to hold pigment
  2. Begin blocking in large color areas
  3. Blend colors while fresh
  4. Build up layers gradually
  5. Use paper towels for large blending areas
  6. Add details with sharpened pastel edges
  7. Protect finished work to prevent smudging

Blending Techniques

Intermediate

Mastering Pastel Blending

Blending is the essence of pastel art. Learn various blending methods to create smooth transitions and beautiful color effects.

Blending Tools & Methods:

  • Blending stumps (tortillons) - precision blending
  • Soft brushes - for larger areas
  • Cotton balls or felt - smooth blending
  • Fingers - natural control (use gloves)
  • Paper towels - soft, gentle blending

Blending Process:

  1. Apply pastel colors side by side
  2. Choose appropriate blending tool for area size
  3. Blend with gentle circular motions
  4. Work colors together smoothly
  5. Don't over-blend or colors become muddy
  6. Preserve some color vibrance
  7. Add highlights and details after blending

Pastel Art Tips & Tricks

Paper Surface

Use paper with enough texture (tooth) to hold pastel particles.

Light to Dark

Always work from light to dark colors to avoid muddy mixing.

Preserve Whites

Save white paper for highlights - don't cover everything.

Fixative Use

Use light, even coats of fixative to protect your artwork.
