Pouring Technique

Beginner

Acrylic Pouring Basics

Acrylic pouring is a fluid art technique where you pour liquid acrylic paint onto a canvas to create mesmerizing patterns and beautiful color combinations. It's accessible to beginners yet offers endless creative possibilities.

Materials Needed:

  • Acrylic paints (fluid or heavy-body)
  • Pouring medium (increases flow)
  • Canvas or wooden board
  • Cups for mixing
  • Sticks for stirring
  • Palette knives
  • Protective coverings

Pouring Process:

  1. Mix paint with pouring medium to achieve right consistency
  2. Tilt canvas to allow paint to flow
  3. Pour colors onto canvas in desired pattern
  4. Tilt and swirl to blend colors
  5. Use palette knives for additional effects
  6. Allow to dry completely (24-48 hours)

Dry Brush Technique

Intermediate

Creating Texture with Dry Brush

The dry brush technique in acrylics creates bold, textured strokes perfect for landscapes, trees, and expressive abstract work. Use minimal water for maximum impact.

What You'll Need:

  • Heavy-body acrylic paints
  • Stiff-bristled brushes
  • Canvas or paper
  • Paper towel for removing excess water

Dry Brush Method:

  1. Load brush with thick paint and minimal water
  2. Wipe off excess on paper towel
  3. Apply with quick, confident strokes
  4. Vary brush direction for natural texture
  5. Build layers for more dramatic effects
  6. Acrylic dries fast, allowing quick layering

Splattering & Dripping

Intermediate

Expressive Splatter Painting

Splattering and dripping creates dynamic, energetic effects in acrylic paintings. These techniques add spontaneity and movement to your artwork.

Tools for Splattering:

  • Old toothbrushes
  • Sticks or palette knives
  • Brushes with stiff bristles
  • Thin consistency acrylic paint
  • Protective coverings for work area

Splatter Application:

  1. Prepare your workspace with protection
  2. Thin paint with water to splatter consistency
  3. Use toothbrush and stick for controlled splatters
  4. Flick brush bristles with stick for random patterns
  5. Allow drips to flow naturally down canvas
  6. Layer different colors for complexity
  7. Can combine with other techniques

Acrylic Painting Tips & Tricks

Fast Drying

Acrylics dry fast - work quickly or use a drying retarder to slow the process.

Opacity Control

Layer thick paint for opacity, or thin it for transparency and glazing effects.

Versatile Surfaces

Paint on canvas, paper, wood, or even fabric with the right preparation.

Clean as You Go

Clean brushes immediately - acrylics won't wash out once dried.
